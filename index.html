FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

All your solar questions, answered.

A residential solar system in Pune typically costs ₹45,000 - ₹55,000 per kW after subsidy. For a 3kW system (suitable for ₹2,000-3,000 monthly bill), the cost is approximately ₹1.35-1.65 lakhs after government subsidy of up to ₹78,000.

Under PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana, you can get up to ₹78,000 subsidy for residential solar. For 1-2kW systems, subsidy is ₹30,000/kW. For 2-3kW, it's ₹18,000/kW (above 2kW). We handle the complete subsidy application process for you.

Most of our customers save 80-100% on their electricity bills. A 3kW system generates approximately 12-14 units per day, which is enough for a household with ₹2,000-3,000 monthly bill. Many customers bring their bill down to ₹0-200.

Physical installation takes 1-2 days for residential systems. The complete process including site survey, design, installation, net metering, and commissioning takes 15-30 days. We handle all paperwork and approvals.

Net metering allows you to export excess solar power to the grid and earn credits. A bidirectional meter tracks both import and export. At the end of the billing cycle, you only pay for the net difference. We handle the complete MSEDCL net metering application.

Yes! Solar panels generate electricity even on cloudy days, though at reduced capacity (30-50% of peak). Pune gets 300+ sunny days per year, making it excellent for solar. Annual generation remains consistent and predictable.

Solar panels come with a 25-year performance warranty and can last 30-35 years. They degrade at only 0.5-0.7% per year. Inverters typically last 10-15 years. We provide 35+ years of service support including maintenance.
